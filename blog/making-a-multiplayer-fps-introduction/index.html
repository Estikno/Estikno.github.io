Introduction

Welcome to the first installment of my blog series, where I’ll take you on an exciting journey through the creation of my very own online first-person shooter (FPS) game! As an avid gamer and a huge fan of FPS games, I’ve always been captivated by games such as CSGO, Valorant, Call of Duty, etc. Now, I’ve decided to embark on a new adventure - building my own FPS game from scratch.

Choosing Unity as the Game Engine:

After careful consideration and research, I decided to build my game using Unity, one of the most popular and versatile game engines in the industry. Unity’s user-friendly interface, extensive documentation, and robust community support made it the ideal choice for a beginner like me. Moreover, its cross-platform capabilities will allow players to enjoy the game on various devices.

Introducing Riptide:

For the multiplayer aspect of my FPS game, I’ll be utilizing a new library called Riptide, developed by the talented Tom Weilland. This library is designed to be used in multiplayer games as well as other applications using .NET. The library gives you a low level control of the network stuff withouth making you write the code for the most basic and frustrating things such as connections and its different protocols, handling messages and much more.

Connection Between Server and Clients:

One of the foundational elements that I’ve already set up is the connection between the server and clients. Ensuring a stable and reliable network architecture is crucial for delivering an enjoyable online gaming experience. With Riptide’s support and Unity’s networking capabilities, I’ve established the groundwork for a robust communication system that will handle player interactions and data synchronization.

What to Expect in This Blog Series:

Throughout this blog series, I’ll be sharing every step of my game development process. From the initial concept and design phase to coding, artwork, level creation, playtesting, and debugging - you’ll witness the challenges and triumphs of my journey. I’ll also document the lessons I learn, the resources I find invaluable, and any tips and tricks that could be useful for fellow aspiring game developers.

It is important to understand that this series is not a tutorial one. However you will see how it is to work behind a project like this one.

Conclusion:

Stay tuned for the upcoming posts, as we dive into the nitty-gritty of game development and take this exhilarating journey together! If you’re a fellow FPS enthusiast or an aspiring game developer, I hope this series will inspire and entertain you.

I intend to share updates and progress on this project every two weeks, as long as my development process allows for it.

Thank you for joining me on this adventure, and I look forward to your support and feedback throughout this exciting process!
